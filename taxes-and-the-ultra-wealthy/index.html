Taxes and the Ultra Wealthy

ProPublica got access to 4 years of tax returns of some of the richest people in the U.S.

While people who live off their income might typically pay 14% of their income in Federal taxes

14%

ProPublica found the richest 25 people -- who live off their wealth -- paid a true tax rate of only 3.4%

But don't the rich also have expenses they can't pay for with stock options too? Glad you asked...

Normally when people sell investments - like stocks - they pay taxes on the money they earned on that investment. But like with many things, there are ways to get around that.

In the simplified scenario below our Billionaire CEO started out 2015 with $5 million invested in each of 10 different stocks. (These 10 stocks were all among the 100 largest companies at the time).

Who You Are

CEO of a massive corporation.

Mission

Buy yourself a $8 million yacht without needing to pay taxes on money you made from your investments.

See your investments over time

Taxes You Owe

0%

Sweet, your $50 Million is now worth $178 Million. Now sell some stocks to buy your yacht. Hover over a stock to see its current value, click to sell that stock.

WELL PLAYED!!

[in actual version this would only show up after you selected the losing stock]

The $50 Million you started with across these 10 stocks is now worth $178 Million, but by selling a losing investment along with a neutral one, you can not only free up some cash for your yacht, but you can claim investment "losses" on your taxes.

In fact, this might be a good time to exercise some of those stock options -- since you can offset those earnings with your investment "losses" and avoid taxation you'd otherwise have to pay when you used your stock option.
